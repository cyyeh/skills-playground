Trade-offs & Limitations

Strengths

  • ✅
    Open source and vendor-neutralApache 2.0 license with Linux Foundation governance. No lock-in to any cloud provider. You own your data and infrastructure.
  • ✅
    Low barrier to entrypip install mlflow and mlflow.autolog() gets you experiment tracking in minutes. No infrastructure required to start.
  • ✅
    Framework agnosticWorks with any ML library. The model flavor system abstracts away framework differences for deployment.
  • ✅
    Massive community800+ contributors, 18k+ GitHub stars, 25M+ monthly downloads. Extensive third-party integrations and community support.
  • ✅
    Full lifecycle coverageTracking, model packaging, registry, serving, and (with 3.x) GenAI observability in a single platform.
  • ✅
    GenAI-native (3.x)First-class tracing, LLM evaluation, and AI Gateway. Not a bolted-on afterthought -- deeply integrated.

Limitations

  • ⚠
    No built-in authenticationThe open-source server has no access control. Production deployments require a reverse proxy with external auth.
  • ⚠
    Not a pipeline orchestratorMLflow tracks experiments but doesn't schedule or orchestrate training pipelines. You need Airflow, Prefect, or Kubeflow for DAG workflows.
  • ⚠
    UI performance at scaleThe tracking UI can slow down with thousands of runs, especially with file-based backends. Even database-backed stores have limits.
  • ⚠
    Single-writer artifact limitationConcurrent artifact writes from multiple processes can cause conflicts with file-based stores. Remote stores handle this better.
  • ⚠
    Limited real-time monitoringDesigned for experiment logging, not real-time model monitoring. Pair with Evidently, WhyLabs, or Arize for drift detection.

MLflow vs. Weights & Biases

AspectMLflowW&B
LicenseOpen source (Apache 2.0)Proprietary (free tier)
HostingSelf-hosted or DatabricksSaaS-first (self-hosted available)
UI / VisualizationFunctional but basicRich, polished, collaborative
Model RegistryFull-featured with aliasesArtifact versioning, less governance
GenAI SupportExtensive (tracing, eval, gateway)W&B Weave for tracing
CostFree (infra costs only)$50-200/user/month for teams
Data OwnershipFull (your infrastructure)W&B servers (or self-hosted)

Choose MLflow when: data sovereignty matters, you need full model lifecycle governance, you want vendor neutrality, or you're in a Databricks ecosystem.

Choose W&B when: you want best-in-class visualization and collaboration UX, your team is small-to-medium, and you prefer SaaS simplicity.

MLflow vs. Kubeflow

AspectMLflowKubeflow
Primary FocusTracking + model managementPipeline orchestration + training
InfrastructureLightweight (Python process)Kubernetes-native (heavy)
Pipeline OrchestrationNoCore strength (Kubeflow Pipelines)
Distributed TrainingTracks results onlyManages (PyTorchJob, etc.)
Model ServingBasic built-in + integrationsKServe (production-grade)
Setup TimeMinutesDays to weeks

Choose MLflow when: you need experiment tracking and model management without heavy infrastructure overhead.

Choose Kubeflow when: you need Kubernetes-native pipeline orchestration and distributed training. Many teams use both.

💡
The pragmatic recommendation: MLflow is the best starting point for most ML teams. Its low barrier to entry, vendor neutrality, and full lifecycle coverage make it a safe foundational choice. As teams scale, they layer complementary tools on top — a pipeline orchestrator, a feature store, a monitoring tool — rather than replacing MLflow.
