Honest Take

DuckDB occupies a unique niche: it brings data warehouse-grade analytical performance to the individual developer's laptop, notebook, or application. Its closest analogy is SQLite -- not because they solve the same problem, but because they share the philosophy of making powerful database technology effortlessly accessible.

🔭
The right choice when: You are a single user or small team analyzing datasets that fit on one machine (up to ~100-200 GB), and you value simplicity, speed, and zero operational overhead over distributed scaling and multi-user concurrency.
💡
Best practice: Use DuckDB and a cloud warehouse together -- DuckDB for fast local development, testing, and ad-hoc exploration; the cloud warehouse for production workloads, multi-user access, and governance. Tools like MotherDuck, dbt-duckdb, and Ibis make this dual-use pattern practical.
